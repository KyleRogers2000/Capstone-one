Summary

Prickly by nature but sharp in business, I’m a dedicated British cactus farmer with a passion for plants that thrive on neglect—much like my sense of humor. I’ve spent years cultivating a thriving desert paradise in the heart of the English countryside, where the rain politely minds its own business (most of the time). Whether it’s taming rogue succulents, hosting “hug-a-cactus” workshops (waivers required), or negotiating with particularly stubborn aloe vera, I’ve got the green thumb and thorn-proof gloves to handle it all. Looking to branch out and bring my unique expertise to new soil—just don’t ask me to water the office plants.

Education

  • Diploma in Horticulture (Specializing in Arid Plants) Royal Botanic Gardens, Kew 2015 – 2017

    Learned to differentiate between a cactus and an overly defensive houseplant. Graduated with honors in “Avoiding Spiky Situations.”

  • Certificate in Greenhouse Management University of Exeter 2013 – 2014

    Specialized in creating microclimates where cacti feel like they’re on holiday in the Sahara. Final project: “How Not to Overwater… Anything.”

  • Advanced Prickly Plant Handling (Safety First!) Online Cactus Safety Academy 2018 (Weekend Crash Course – survived)

    Mastered the art of repotting without requiring first aid. Successfully completed module: “When Aloe Vera Becomes Your Best Friend.”

  • BSc in Agricultural Science University of Reading 2010 – 2013

    Dissertation: “Cactus Cultivation in the UK: When It’s Less Rain, More Gain.” Top of the class in “Plant Survival Against All Odds.”

Work Experience

  1. Head Cactus Wrangler & Farm Manager The Thorny Patch, Cornwall 2018 – Present

    Cultivated a thriving collection of over 500 species of cacti and succulents, all with a mutual dislike of British weather. Hosted workshops teaching brave participants how to handle cacti with minimal bloodshed. Implemented innovative irrigation systems that water plants just enough to keep them guessing.

  2. Greenhouse Supervisor Succulent Sanctum, Devon 2015 – 2018

    Managed climate control systems to keep plants toasty without roasting them. Developed a revolutionary “Don’t Touch That!” policy for overly curious visitors. Successfully led a team of apprentices through the trials and tribulations of thorn management.

  3. Plant Care Specialist Royal Botanic Gardens, Kew 2013 – 2015

    Maintained arid plant exhibits and kept cacti in tip-top, needle-sharp shape. Provided emergency care to sunburnt succulents and rehydration therapy to wilting aloe. Assisted in research on adapting desert plants to damp British conditions—results inconclusive, but hopeful.

  4. Part-Time Garden Centre Assistant Bloomin’ Marvellous Garden Centre, Surrey 2010 – 2013

    Educated confused customers on why their cactus was turning into a mushy mess (hint: too much love and water). Oversaw the “Adopt-a-Cactus” program, pairing plants with responsible owners. Maintained inventory with the precision of a gardener avoiding eye contact with a particularly spiky agave.
